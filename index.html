Tourist Sites of Valencia

  • The City of Arts and Sciences: This architectural complex is one of the most popular attractions in Valencia. It is made up of several futuristic buildings that house a planetarium, an aquarium, a science museum, a theater and a botanical garden.
  • Valencia Cathedral: Also known as the Cathedral of Santa María, it is one of the main places of worship in the city. It stands out for its mix of architectural styles, ranging from Gothic to Baroque.
  • The Silk Exchange: This historic building is an example of Valencian Gothic civil architecture. It was built in the 15th century and was used as a meeting place for the city's silk merchants.
  • The Central Market: It is one of the largest markets in Europe and has a wide variety of fresh products. It is an ideal place to taste the typical products of Valencian gastronomy.
  • The neighborhood of Carmen: It is one of the oldest neighborhoods in the city and has numerous narrow streets and charming squares. It is ideal for walking and discovering the history of Valencia.
  • The Beaches of Valencia: The city has several beaches that are ideal for relaxing and enjoying the sun and the sea. Some of the most popular are Playa de la Malvarrosa, Playa del Cabanyal and Playa de las Arenas.
  • Mercado de Colón:This beautiful modernist building is home to a gourmet food market, as well as a variety of restaurants, cafes, and shops. It's a great place to grab a bite to eat or do some shopping.
  • Torres de Serranos: These Gothic-style towers are part of the old city walls of Valencia and offer great views of the city from the top. They are open daily and admission is around 2 euros.
  • La Almoina Archaeological Center:This museum is located in the heart of the city and showcases the archaeological remains of Valencia's Roman and Moorish past. It's open Tuesday to Sunday and admission is around 2 euros.
  • Plaza del Ayuntamiento:This is the main square in Valencia and is home to the beautiful City Hall building. It's a great place to people-watch and take in the sights and sounds of the city.
  • La Ciudadela: This historic fortress was built in the 16th century and is now home to a beautiful park and gardens. It's open daily and admission is free.
